Determining the needs and resources of schools in your community
is an important startingpoint for any school-based service project.
A thorough needs assessment not only helps you identify the best
project but , by including students, parents, teachers, and administrators
in the process, also ensures support for your endavours.

To help you determine the weakness and strength of your schools, we've
complied a listof questions for you to ask your schools' stakeholders
These are omly suggestions; you may wish to ask additional questions
or adaptthese questions to fit the needs of your local community.

Background

Questions for students

  • How old are you?
  • What languages do you speak?
  • How often do you and your teachers
    work together? once a day? once a week?
  • What is your favourite school event?
  • Are you involved in the student government at your school?

Questions for parents

  • How often do you meet with your child's
    teachers?
  • How often do you receive communication
    from your child's school?
    And in what language do yourecieve the communication?
  • What is the primary language spoken at home?
  • What are the most important school events for
    you and your child during the academic year?
  • Are you involved in your school's parent-teacher organisation?
  • Do you volunteer in your child's classroom? If yes,
    what volunteer activities are you involved in?
    How much time do you spend volunteering at school?

  • How often do you communicate with the parents
    of your students?
  • How do you communicate with parents and strudents?
  • How do you involve parents in the classroom?
  • What are the more important events for you and
    your students during the academic year?
  • In what ways are parents and students involved

  • How could parents and students help improve

Questions for administrators

  • How many of your students are male

School Environment

Questions for students, parents,
teachers, and administraters

  • What do you like most about your school?
    Why do you like these things?
  • If you could change something about your school
    what would you change and why?
  • Is your school building in good condition?
  • Are the school grounds attractive or neat?
  • How are you involved in improving your school's
    environment?
  • In your opinion,are students safe in your
    school? If not, what would make your school safer?

School and Classroom Resources

Questions for students

  • Do you have your own books in class?
  • What books do you use in your classes?
  • What books do you enjoy reading the most?
    Why do you enjoy them?
  • Do you ever use computers in your classes?
    If yes, how do you use them?
  • Do you have enough pens, pencils, and paper
    for class?
  • What school supplies do you wish you had that
    you don't?
  • Do you ever use your school library? If yes,
    what do you do there?
  • What item or items do you wish your clssroom
    or library had? And why?

Questions for parents

  • What books do you enjoy reading to your children
    Why do you like these books?
  • Does your child use computers at school?
    If yes, how are they used?
  • Does your child use a computer at home or
    help with housework?
  • Does your child have his/her own school books
    or are books shared with other students?
  • If you could add something to improve your child's
    classroom or library,whta would you add and why?
  • How do you feel about volunteers helping students
    in your child's classroom?
  • In an average year, how much money do you spend
    on school materials for your child?

    need the most?

  • Do all of your students have books? If not,

    in the classroom?
  • What professional development opportunites

  • How could your school library be improved?

Questions for administraters

    students and teachers at your school?
  • What additional resources do students and
